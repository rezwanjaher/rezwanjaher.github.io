Featured Research

Deconstructing Intraocular Pressure: A Non-invasive Multi-Stage Probabilistic Inverse Framework

Pre-print ArXiv 2509.14167 GitHub Code INFORMS Manuscript [PDF]

Md Rezwan Jaher, Abul Mukid Mohammad Mukaddes, A. B. M. Abdul Malek
September 17, 2025

Abstract

Many critical healthcare decisions are challenged by the inability to measure key underlying parameters. Glaucoma, a leading cause of irreversible blindness driven by elevated intraocular pressure (IOP), provides a stark example. The primary determinant of IOP, a tissue property called trabecular meshwork permeability, cannot be measured in vivo, forcing clinicians to depend on indirect surrogates. This clinical challenge is compounded by a broader computational one: developing predictive models for such ill-posed inverse problems is hindered by a lack of ground-truth data and prohibitive cost of large-scale, high-fidelity simulations. We address both challenges with an end-to-end framework to noninvasively estimate unmeasurable variables from sparse, routine data. Our approach combines a multi-stage artificial intelligence architecture to functionally separate the problem; a novel data generation strategy we term PCDS that obviates the need for hundreds of thousands of costly simulations, reducing the effective computational time from years to hours; and a Bayesian engine to quantify predictive uncertainty. Our framework deconstructs a single IOP measurement into its fundamental components, yielding estimates for the unmeasurable tissue permeability and a patient's outflow facility. Validation against diverse clinical data confirmed the framework's high fidelity. Our noninvasive estimates of outflow facility achieved excellent agreement with state-of-the-art tonography, showing near-zero bias and precision comparable to direct physical instruments. Furthermore, the newly derived permeability biomarker demonstrates high accuracy in stratifying clinical cohorts by disease risk, highlighting its diagnostic potential. This work delivers a computational tool for clinical decision support by providing a complete, probabilistic profile of a patient's condition. This profile visualizes the entire distribution of plausible hydrodynamic states consistent with routine inputs (age and IOP), offering a holistic view unattainable with current methods and democratizing access to advanced diagnostics. More broadly, our framework establishes a generalizable blueprint for solving similar inverse problems in other data-scarce, computationally-intensive domains.

This project introduces an end-to-end computational framework from ground-truth dataset generation to patient-specific inference for the purpose of solving a long-standing, ill-posed, and underdetermined inverse problem in ocular hydrodynamics using only routine clinical input.

Methodological Contributions

  • Developed a novel, physics-informed, multi-stage artificial intelligence (AI) architecture to make the underdetermined problem tractable by separating biophysical and patient-specific components.
  • Engineered a new hybrid data generation strategy, Physics-Calibrated Data Scaling (PCDS), which enabled the creation of a massive (n=120,000), physically-consistent training dataset. This strategy reduced computational time on a consumer-grade CPU from an estimated multi-year runtime to under 8 hours.
  • Enabled robust inference from sparse clinical inputs (age, IOP) using a constrained Monte Carlo simulation to propagate prior uncertainty and generate full posterior biomarker distributions.

Clinical & Scientific Impact

  • Delivered, to our knowledge, the first-ever estimates of patient-specific trabecular meshwork permeability (KTM), a critical biomarker of glaucoma pathology previously considered unmeasurable in vivo.
  • Produced, to our knowledge, the first computational tool to estimate outflow facility, the current clinical gold standard, from routine data alone (patient's age and IOP). Our framework inferred outflow facility, achieved precision and near-zero bias comparable to state-of-the-art physical tonography instruments.
  • Established the first framework to deconstruct a single clinical measurement of IOP into a full probabilistic profile, laying down the foundation for a possible shift from surrogate-based monitoring to a direct, mechanistic assessment of disease.

This research culminated in a first-author manuscript, submitted to the INFORMS Journal on Computing.

Algorithm 1: Patient-Specific Hydrodynamic Profiling Framework
  1. Input: Patient Age Agep, Intraocular Pressure IOPp
  2. Output: Synthesized profile with posterior distributions for {KTM, G, Ctrab, Dp} and constrained prior distributions for {QAH, Fu, EVP}
  3. Generate N = 1000 plausible hydrodynamic state vectors {Xi}Ni=1 by sampling physiological priors {QAH, Fu, EVP}i from age-stratified distributions P(Agep).
  4. For each state i, set Xi = {IOPp, QAH,i, Fu,i, EVPi, Agep}.
  5. for i = 1 → N do
  6. Infer permeability KTM,i ← AISolver(Xi).
  7. Construct feature vector Zi = {KTM,i, Xi}.
  8. Infer geometry factor Gi ← PatientSpecificCalibrationBridge(Zi).
  9. end for
  10. Synthesize full posterior distributions for KTM from {KTM,i}Ni=1 and G from {Gi}Ni=1.
  11. Derive posterior for Ctrab by applying the composite relationship to the full distributions of KTM and G.
  12. Derive posterior for Dp from the KTM distribution using the Kozeny-Carman relation and age-dependent porosity ε(Agep).
  13. return All posterior and constrained prior distributions.

Ongoing Research

Learning Scalable Policies for Invasive Species Management: A Deep Reinforcement Learning Framework for Spatio-Temporal POMDPs

Technical Report [PDF] GitHub Code

Md Rezwan Jaher
Jul. 2025 - Present

  • Developed a novel “glass-box” Stochastic Program Emulation Environment (SPEE), a high-fidelity simulator grounded in the first-principles dynamics of state-of-the-art M-SMIP model.
  • Trained a Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) agent using a Graph Neural Network (GNN) architecture, to learn a scalable and adaptive control policy that overcomes the “curse of dimensionality” inherent in traditional optimization.
  • Validated the learned policy against the provably optimal M-SMIP solution on a solvable benchmark and demonstrated its power on a computationally intractable instance by converting a projected $2.05M loss (worst baseline heuristic) into a $1.81M net benefit.
  • Extensions and further improvements work in progress. A comprehensive technical report detailing the complete methodology, validation, and results of current progress is available online. [PDF]
